what_i_learned: |
  Today was the official first day of the program, and we started with a full onboarding session that introduced us to the research environment, expectations, and overall structure of the program. I met my research group for the first time, along with my mentor, and it felt both exciting and slightly overwhelming stepping into a new academic space.

  We went over how the program will be structured week by week, including weekly check-ins, progress updates, and how our daily blog entries will be used to document our learning journey. I also learned how important communication and consistency will be throughout the program, especially when working in a research team setting.

  In addition, we were introduced to the main tools we will be using during the program, including GitHub for tracking work, Slack for communication, and the lab calendar for scheduling meetings and deadlines. At first, I felt a little nervous because I am still getting used to all these platforms, but the orientation helped make things feel more clear and manageable.

  We also briefly discussed how to approach research thinking in general—being curious, asking questions, and not being afraid to explore ideas even if they are not fully developed yet. That part made me feel more confident because it reminded me that learning is a process and it is okay to start without knowing everything.

blockers: |
  Right now, I do not have any major blockers related to the actual research content, since we are still in the early onboarding stage. However, I am still adjusting to all the new tools and systems being used in the program.

  GitHub feels a bit new and technical to me, and I am still figuring out how to properly navigate it and understand how it will be used for my work. Slack is easier, but I am still getting used to the communication flow and making sure I do not miss important messages or updates.

  Overall, these are not serious issues, just part of the learning process as I get familiar with everything.

reflection: |
  Today made me feel a mix of nervousness, excitement, and curiosity. Walking into a new research environment and meeting new people always feels a little intimidating at first, but everyone seemed welcoming and supportive, which made the experience much more comfortable.

  I felt especially excited when we started talking about the research goals and what we will be working on over the next few weeks. It made everything feel real and meaningful, and I am looking forward to building my skills and learning how research is actually done in a structured setting.

  Even though I felt a bit overwhelmed at times with all the new information, I also felt motivated because I know this is part of the learning process. I am trying to remind myself that it is okay not to understand everything immediately.

  Tomorrow, I am planning to start my literature review and begin working on a rough draft of my project proposal with my mentor. I am looking forward to taking the first real step into my research topic and becoming more comfortable with the tools and workflow.
