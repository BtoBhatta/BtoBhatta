what_i_learned: |
  Today we discussed the datasets that had been successfully downloaded and began
  comparing the results from our fairness evaluations. We reviewed how different
  demographic groups were represented in the data and examined whether the model's
  performance varied across those groups. This helped me better understand why
  fairness is an important aspect of machine learning, especially in healthcare
  applications where biased predictions can negatively affect patient outcomes.

  Our mentor carefully explained the terminology used in fairness analysis, including
  concepts such as bias, fairness metrics, demographic groups, subgroup performance,
  false positives, false negatives, and performance disparities. We also discussed
  how these metrics help identify whether a model treats different populations
  equitably. The explanations connected the theoretical concepts from the research
  papers to the practical results we observed, making it much easier to interpret
  our findings and understand the purpose of fairness evaluation.
