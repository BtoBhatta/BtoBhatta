what_i_learned: |
  Today I trained our baseline 1D-CNN model using the PTB-XL ECG dataset. Before training, I reviewed how the dataset was prepared by mapping ECG diagnosis codes into five diagnostic classes, creating the official train, validation, and test splits, and loading the ECG waveforms into PyTorch DataLoaders.

  I also learned why setting a global random seed is important for reproducibility so that the same experiments can be repeated with consistent results. During training, I observed how the model processed batches of ECG recordings, calculated prediction errors using Binary Cross-Entropy Loss, updated its weights through backpropagation, and improved over ten training epochs.

  After each epoch, I monitored both the training and validation loss to evaluate how well the model generalized to unseen data. The training loss steadily decreased throughout the process, showing that the model was successfully learning meaningful ECG features. Once training was complete, I saved the trained model checkpoint so it can be reused without retraining.

  Finally, I reviewed the fairness evaluation phase, which measures the model's False Negative Rate across different age and sex demographic groups. This helped me understand how fairness metrics can identify whether certain patient groups are more likely to receive missed diagnoses, which is an important consideration when developing trustworthy AI systems for healthcare.

blockers: |
  Training the model required a significant amount of time because each epoch processed thousands of ECG recordings. I also spent time understanding how the training loop, validation process, and fairness evaluation worked together before moving on to the next stage of the project.
