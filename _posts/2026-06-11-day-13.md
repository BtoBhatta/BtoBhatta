what_i_learned: |
Today we started our day in breakout rooms with our mentors and teammates, continuing our work on the PTB-XL ECG dataset and our deep learning models. We reviewed the progress we had made on the project and discussed the next steps for developing our Hybrid CNN-Transformer architecture. The breakout room discussions were very helpful because they allowed us to collaborate, ask questions, and share ideas about the project.

A major focus of today's session was continuing the development of our Hybrid CNN-Transformer model. We learned how convolutional neural networks can be used to extract important ECG features while transformer layers help capture long-range relationships within the signal. Our mentors explained how combining these two approaches can potentially improve ECG classification performance compared to using a traditional CNN alone.

Throughout the day, we spent a significant amount of time coding in Google Colab. We worked on implementing the model architecture, reviewing each section of the code, and understanding how the data flows through the network. We explored different layers such as convolutional layers, pooling layers, transformer encoder layers, and classification layers. This helped me gain a better understanding of how deep learning models process ECG data.

We also performed architecture sanity checks by creating dummy ECG batches and passing them through both our baseline 1D-CNN model and the Hybrid CNN-Transformer model. This allowed us to verify that the models were producing outputs with the correct dimensions. Through this process, I learned more about tensor shapes, feature extraction, and how to troubleshoot coding issues when building machine learning models.

During the afternoon, we continued collaborating with our peers and mentors while refining our code and discussing future improvements to the project. The hands-on coding experience helped reinforce many of the concepts we have been learning throughout the internship and gave me more confidence working with PyTorch and deep learning architectures.
