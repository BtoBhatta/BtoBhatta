Today started with a breakout room session where we continued working on our Hybrid Transformer and 1D-CNN models for the ECG classification project. We discussed our progress, reviewed the architecture of both models, and collaborated on understanding how the different components work together.

During the coding session, we focused on building and implementing the Hybrid CNN-Transformer model in Google Colab. I learned more about how convolutional layers extract important ECG features while transformer layers help capture long-range relationships within the signal. It was interesting to see how both approaches can be combined into a single model.

We spent a significant amount of time coding, testing different sections of the model, and making sure the architecture was functioning correctly. We performed model sanity checks using dummy ECG data and verified that the outputs had the correct dimensions. This helped me better understand how data flows through deep learning models and how tensor shapes change at each stage of the network.

Throughout the day, I also learned more about PyTorch, neural network layers, feature extraction, and transformer encoders. Although some concepts were challenging, the hands-on coding experience helped reinforce what we have been learning throughout the program.
