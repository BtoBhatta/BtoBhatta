what_i_learned: |
  Today was one of the most exciting and enjoyable days of the research program.
  Instead of spending the day coding or writing, our team focused on filming our
  90-second elevator pitch video. We began by reviewing the script we had written
  yesterday and assigning everyone their roles. We practiced several times before
  recording to make sure our timing, expressions, and dialogue flowed naturally.

  Our video was designed as a short, engaging skit to explain our research project.
  One of our teammates played the role of a patient, another was a family member,
  and I played the doctor explaining the patient's ECG results and how artificial
  intelligence can assist with diagnosis. Through the skit, we highlighted the
  importance of our project and explained why fairness in AI matters, showing that
  an AI model should perform reliably for people of different ages and sexes rather
  than favoring one group over another.

  We recorded multiple scenes from different angles and repeated several takes to
  improve our delivery and make the video more engaging. There were plenty of funny
  moments during filming, especially when someone forgot their lines or we started
  laughing in the middle of a scene. Although it took most of the day to complete,
  everyone stayed enthusiastic and worked together to make the final video as
  polished as possible.

  Today's activity helped me realize that communicating research is just as
  important as conducting it. Creating a short video that explains a complex topic
  in a simple and engaging way required creativity, teamwork, and effective
  communication. It was a fun experience and one of my favorite days of the
  internship so far.

blockers: |
  The biggest challenge was getting the timing and dialogue right within the
  90-second limit. We had to record several takes to improve our delivery and
  ensure the message was clear, but working together helped us overcome these
  challenges.
