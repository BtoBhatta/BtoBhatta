Today we started our day in breakout rooms with our mentors and teammates, continuing our work on the PTB-XL ECG dataset and the 1D Convolutional Neural Network (1D-CNN) model. We spent time reviewing the dataset, discussing how the ECG signals are processed, and working through different sections of the model code. This helped reinforce my understanding of how neural networks learn patterns from ECG data and how different layers contribute to the model's predictions.

Throughout the morning, we practiced coding in Google Colab and continued building our knowledge of the 1D-CNN architecture. We discussed concepts such as feature extraction, convolutional layers, and how the model processes ECG signals to identify important patterns.

Later in the day, Dr. Pandey gave a presentation on literature reviews and research paper writing. He explained the importance of finding high-quality research articles related to our project and showed us how to search for relevant papers using tools such as Google Scholar. We also reviewed several research articles and were assigned the task of finding papers that use methodologies similar to our project so that we can begin contributing to the writing process.

During our afternoon session, our faculty mentor shared several coding resources and practice documents that made learning Python and machine learning concepts more approachable. These materials provided additional examples that helped clarify many of the concepts we have been studying. We then continued working on our 1D-CNN model and discussed the next steps for model development and project progress.
