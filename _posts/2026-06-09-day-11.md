what_i_learned: |
Today we started our day in breakout rooms with our mentors and teammates, continuing our work on the PTB-XL ECG dataset and our 1D Convolutional Neural Network (1D-CNN) model. We spent time reviewing the dataset, discussing how ECG signals are processed, and practicing coding in Google Colab. This helped me better understand how the model learns patterns from ECG data and how different components of the architecture work together.

Throughout the morning, we continued exploring the 1D-CNN model and worked through coding exercises related to the project. We discussed concepts such as feature extraction, convolutional layers, and how neural networks process ECG signals to make predictions.

Later in the day, Dr. Pandey explained the importance of literature reviews and research paper writing. He showed us how to search for relevant research articles and discussed how previous studies can help guide our project. We reviewed several articles related to ECG classification and machine learning, and we were assigned the task of finding papers that use methodologies similar to ours. These papers may also be sent to the Writing Center for additional feedback and support.

During our afternoon session, our faculty mentor shared several helpful coding documents and practice resources with our team. These materials made it easier to understand coding concepts and provided additional opportunities to practice Python and machine learning techniques. We then returned to working on our 1D-CNN model and continued making progress on the project.
