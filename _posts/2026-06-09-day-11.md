what_i_learned: |
Today started with our team working in breakout rooms on the PTB-XL ECG dataset and continuing development of our 1D Convolutional Neural Network (1D-CNN) model. We focused on understanding the model architecture, including convolutional layers, pooling layers, activation functions, and how ECG data flows through the network. We also spent time coding in Google Colab and reviewing how the model processes ECG signals to learn important patterns such as P waves, QRS complexes, and T waves.

Throughout the coding session, we examined tensor shapes, feature extraction, and how different layers transform the ECG signal before making predictions. This helped me better understand how neural networks are structured and how deep learning models are built using PyTorch.

Later, Dr. Pandey introduced the literature review process and explained how research papers support the development of machine learning projects. We reviewed several research articles related to ECG classification, AI, and deep learning. We were assigned the task of finding papers that use similar methodologies to our project so that we can begin developing the research paper and potentially submit sections to the Writing Center for feedback.

In the afternoon, our faculty mentor shared coding resources and practice documents that simplified many of the concepts we have been learning. These materials provided additional examples and exercises that helped reinforce my understanding of Python, neural networks, and model development. We then continued working on our 1D-CNN implementation and discussed future steps involving model training and evaluation.
