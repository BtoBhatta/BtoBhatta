what_i_learned: |
  Today began with our research lab session, where we continued working on our ECG classification project. We reviewed our recent progress and discussed the next steps for analyzing ECG signals using deep learning techniques. The lab environment provided an opportunity to collaborate with teammates, ask questions, and gain a clearer understanding of how different components of our project fit together.

  After the lab session, we continued our work on GitHub. We explored the repository structure, reviewed project files, and practiced using GitHub as a collaborative development platform. This helped us understand the importance of version control, project organization, and maintaining a record of changes throughout the research process. As our project grows, keeping our work organized through GitHub will be essential for efficient collaboration and reproducibility.

  One of the most valuable parts of the day was the literature review session. During this session, we examined research papers related to ECG classification, deep learning models, and fairness in machine learning. Reading and discussing these papers helped us understand how researchers design experiments, evaluate models, and present their findings. It also exposed us to different approaches used in medical AI and highlighted the importance of considering both predictive performance and fairness when developing clinical decision-support systems. This session broadened my perspective and helped me appreciate how research builds upon existing knowledge.

  We also spent time navigating through Overleaf, which is an important platform for collaborative academic writing. We were assigned tasks and exercises to help us become more comfortable with creating and editing research documents. Learning how to use Overleaf effectively will be useful when writing our literature review, documenting our methodology, and preparing future research reports. Exploring the platform gave me a better understanding of how researchers collaborate on technical documents and manage large writing projects.

  Later in the day, we worked on our weekly showcase presentation. Our team organized the material we had learned throughout the week and prepared slides highlighting our project goals, dataset exploration, literature review findings, and future plans. Presenting our work allowed us to practice communicating technical concepts in a clear and concise manner. It was encouraging to see the progress our team has made and to learn about the work being done by other groups as well.

reflection: |
  Today was both productive and educational because it combined hands-on project work with opportunities to learn from academic research. The literature review session was particularly meaningful because it showed how much effort goes into developing and validating machine learning models for healthcare applications. Reading research papers helped me understand that successful projects require not only technical implementation but also careful evaluation, documentation, and critical thinking.

  Working with GitHub and Overleaf also reinforced the importance of using professional tools throughout the research process. Although these platforms can seem overwhelming at first, becoming familiar with them will help me collaborate more effectively and prepare research documents in a more organized manner.

  The weekly showcase presentation helped me reflect on how much we have accomplished in a relatively short period of time. Looking back at our progress, I feel more confident in my understanding of ECG datasets, machine learning workflows, and the overall research process. The feedback and discussions during the showcase also gave me ideas for areas that we can continue improving in the coming weeks.

blockers: |
  One challenge I faced today was understanding some of the technical concepts presented in the research papers. Many papers use advanced terminology, statistical methods, and deep learning concepts that require additional reading and background knowledge.

  Another challenge was becoming familiar with new research tools such as Overleaf and GitHub. While both platforms are extremely useful, learning their features and workflows takes time and practice.

  Additionally, connecting ideas from the literature to our own project can sometimes be difficult because different studies use different datasets, evaluation methods, and model architectures. Continued practice with reading research papers and discussing them with mentors will help overcome these challenges.
