what_i_learned: |
Today began with a session where we learned about SAND and gained a better understanding of its purpose and how it can support our work throughout the research program. After that, we had a brief introduction to Overleaf, where we explored some of its features and learned how it can be used for writing and collaborating on research papers efficiently.

Later in the day, we participated in a team game activity. It was a very fun and engaging experience that allowed us to work together, communicate, and enjoy some friendly competition. Although our team did not win, everyone had a great time, and the activity helped strengthen team bonding and collaboration.

After the game, we returned to the research lab and continued working on our research paper. We spent time reviewing our progress, organizing our findings, and discussing the next steps for our project. Working on the paper helped us better understand the research process and the importance of clear documentation and communication.

reflection: |
Today was a great balance between learning, teamwork, and research. Learning about SAND and receiving an introduction to Overleaf helped me understand some of the tools that researchers use to collaborate and manage their work. I found it interesting to see how Overleaf simplifies the process of writing and editing research papers with multiple contributors.

The team-building game was one of the most enjoyable parts of the day. Even though our team lost, the activity encouraged communication, problem-solving, and collaboration. It reminded me that teamwork is about supporting one another and learning from the experience rather than focusing only on the outcome.

Another valuable lesson was learning how to fill out timesheets correctly. Understanding how to document work hours accurately is an important professional skill that will be useful throughout the internship and in future careers. Overall, today was productive, enjoyable, and helped me continue developing both my research and professional skills.

Additionally, organizing research findings into a well-structured paper remains challenging at times. As I continue working with my team and gaining experience, I expect these challenges to become easier to manage.
