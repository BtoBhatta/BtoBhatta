what_i_learned: |
  Today I developed a stronger understanding of how literature review shapes the direction of a research project. By studying prior work on the PTB-XL dataset, ECG classification, and fairness in healthcare AI, I learned how researchers build on existing methods rather than starting from scratch.

  I also learned the importance of defining evaluation metrics early in the methodology phase. Metrics such as false positive rate (FPR), false negative rate (FNR), and fairness variability across demographic groups are essential for evaluating model performance beyond simple accuracy.

  Additionally, I gained a better understanding of how 1D-CNN models are applied to ECG signals and how repeated experimental runs can help measure model stability and reliability.

blockers: |
  One of the main challenges today was connecting fairness metrics with predictive stability in a clear and structured way. While both concepts are individually understandable, linking them into a unified experimental framework required deeper discussion and clarification.

  Another difficulty was narrowing down the most relevant papers from a large pool of research. Many studies focused on ECG classification and deep learning, but only a few directly addressed fairness in healthcare AI, which made selection more time-consuming.

  Finally, organizing these ideas into a coherent methodology section in Overleaf required multiple revisions to improve clarity and logical flow.

reflection: |
  Overall, today was a productive and insightful session in the research lab. I feel my understanding of the project has become more structured, especially in how fairness is evaluated in medical AI systems.

  The literature review helped connect individual research ideas into a broader framework and clarified how our project fits within existing work. Even though some parts were initially complex, discussing and breaking them down made the methodology much clearer.

  By the end of the session, I felt more confident about the direction of the project and better prepared for the next stages of model development and evaluation.
