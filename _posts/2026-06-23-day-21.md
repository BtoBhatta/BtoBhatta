what_i_learned: |
 Today I gained a much deeper understanding of how literature review directly shapes the direction of a research project. By carefully studying prior work on PTB-XL, ECG classification, and fairness in healthcare AI, I learned how researchers build upon existing methods rather than starting from scratch.

I also learned how important it is to clearly define evaluation metrics early in the methodology phase. In particular, concepts like false positive rate (FPR), false negative rate (FNR), and fairness variability across demographic groups helped me understand how model performance can differ beyond just accuracy.

Additionally, I developed a better understanding of how 1D-CNN models are applied to ECG signals and how experimental design (such as multiple training runs) can help measure model stability and reliability.

blockers: |
 One of the main challenges today was connecting fairness metrics with predictive stability in a clear and structured way. While both concepts are individually understandable, linking them together in a meaningful experimental framework required deeper discussion and clarification.

Another difficulty was narrowing down the most relevant papers from a large pool of research. Many studies were related to ECG classification and deep learning, but only a few directly focused on fairness and healthcare bias, which made selection more time-consuming.

Lastly, organizing these ideas into a coherent methodology section in Overleaf required multiple revisions to ensure clarity and logical flow.

reflection: |
Overall, today was a highly productive and insightful session. I feel that my understanding of the research topic has become much more structured, especially in terms of how fairness in AI systems is evaluated in medical applications.

Working through the literature helped me see how each paper contributes a specific piece to our overall research question. Even though some parts were initially confusing, discussing and breaking them down made the methodology much clearer.

By the end of the session, I felt more confident about the direction of our project and better prepared to contribute to the next stages of model development and evaluation.
